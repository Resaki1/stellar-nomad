# Star Citizen / CIG Planetary Cloud & Genesis Tech — Web Research Report (verification of CLOUD_TYPES_PLAN.md §3.3)

## SUMMARY

The §3.3 reconstruction is **directionally correct but conflates two different eras**. What actually ships in Star Citizen today (through Alpha 4.8, May 2026) is a raymarched volumetric cloud system (built 2020–2021 by the Frankfurt engine/R&D team, first shipped with Crusader in Alpha 3.14) whose cloud placement is **static and artist-authored** — per-planet custom 3D volume textures, tint, and shaping parameters, with a narrow-band SDF over cloud coverage used for empty-space skipping, temporal reprojection/upsampling, cloud shadow maps, and unified integration into the atmosphere raymarcher. There is **no public evidence of a Nubis-style 2D multi-channel weather map** driving the shipped clouds. Meanwhile, the data-map-driven story ("humidity, temperature, height, wind drive everything including clouds") is the **Genesis / Planet Tech v5 announcement** (CitizenCon 2954, Oct 2024): planetary data sets confirmed as height maps, temperature, humidity, geology, soil type, soil depth/nutrients and offline-simulated erosion data — and CIG stated these same data sets will drive a **physical weather simulation** in which clouds form, move and dissipate based on oceans, prevailing winds, mountain ranges and temperature ("it wasn't really a cloud simulation, it's a weather simulation" — Ali Brown). That system is announced, demoed in controlled captures, and **not yet shipped**.

On the user's specific belief: temperature + humidity are confirmed as the two historic global maps (Planet Tech v4) that drove biome distribution; Genesis adds height maps, geology, soil type/depth/nutrients, erosion data. **Wind is not a stored authoring map** — it appears as a field of the announced runtime weather simulation. Precipitation is an *output* of that simulation, not an input map. Cloud *types* barely exist in CIG's public material: the CitizenCon 2954 demo names only stratus and cumulus, emerging from the simulation rather than from a type channel. For Crusader, the gas giant is rendered with the *same* cloud tech as terrestrial planets (multi-layered, varying-scale, artist-"cloudscaped", deliberately less Earth-like), which validates Stellar Nomad's "same marcher + per-planet config/generator" approach; CIG hand-authored their gas giant rather than deriving it from any weather channels, so the plan's band-table generator goes beyond anything CIG has shown.

Overall: the meteorology-grounded reconstruction in §3.4 (climate predictor fields → derived cloud-control channels) is *more* concrete than anything CIG has published; CIG's announced Genesis weather direction is consistent with it but is a runtime simulation ambition, while Stellar Nomad's baked-map design matches what has actually shipped across the industry.

## Finding 1 — Genesis / Planet Tech v5 data maps: what is confirmed

- Planet Tech v4 (Alpha 3.8, 2019 →): **Temperature and Humidity maps infer biome selection**; artists created biome "slices" distributed across planets via those two properties. CIG explicitly called out at CitizenCon 2954 that using only humidity+temperature "limits to only two degrees of variation" — the motivation for Genesis.
- Genesis / PT v5 (CitizenCon 2954 "Brave New Worlds", Oct 2024): pre-Genesis data was temperature (defined locally + globally) and humidity, "and that's it"; Genesis **adds geology type, soil type, soil depth, soil quality/nutrients** and more; sunlight exposure and slope aspect are *derived*. Erosion simulations are run **offline** to build localized data maps that place rocks/debris/scree.
- CitizenCon 2955 (Oct 2025, "Breaking New Ground") enumerated the Genesis data sets as: **height maps, humidity, temperature, soil type, soil amounts, geology, erosion data** "to name but a few". Height/elevation is thereby confirmed as a first-class dataset. **Wind and precipitation are absent from every published data-map list.**
- These maps drive: terrain texturing (4-layer shader: bedrock/soil/debris), flora placement via per-asset viability/competition rule sets, rock scattering from erosion data, minables distribution, settlement placement (Starchitect queries the same data), NPC/audio systems, and the distant terrain color (planet shader evaluates what *would* spawn — replacing artist LUTs).

## Finding 2 — How Genesis drives clouds: an announced physical weather simulation (NOT shipped)

From the CitizenCon 2954 panel (final "Nature's Wrath" phase, presented by Ali Brown & Ben Pratt/team — transcript):

- Current clouds are described by CIG themselves as **completely static** — authored cloud shapes, never changing.
- Genesis plan: clouds evolve over time, explicitly *not* scrolling animation — the **same physical data sets that drive biomes also drive a physical simulation of the clouds**, so they form and dissipate under realistic conditions; oceans, prevailing winds, mountain ranges and temperature all impact the clouds.
- The demo narrates clouds forming/changing/dissipating "based on wind data — stratus and cumulus alike" (the **only** cloud-type vocabulary in any CIG material; no cirrus, no type channel, no genus system mentioned anywhere).
- A cloud volume's presence "informed by Genesis data" arrives as a **weather front**: wind ramps up (turbulence in the flight model), wet terrain, dynamic puddles, rain shafts with atmospheric scattering under the columns, lightning that damages ships/avionics; storms can be short-lived and localized **or larger and permanent** per planet (gating traversal to ground vehicles — a design/gameplay lever, like Guerrilla's superstorms).
- Alongside: an improved cloud lighting model that correctly accounts for **multiple scattering inside the cloud** (deeper cores, more vivid cloudscapes) and enhanced occlusion/light shafts under cover.
- Status: aired Oct 2024; CitizenCon 2955 (Oct 2025) said Genesis "comes to the persistent universe starting next year" and its Genesis panel covered **terrain/biomes only — zero cloud/weather content**. Alpha 4.8.0 (May 2026) patch notes contain no dynamic weather or Genesis clouds. The Star Citizen Wiki's Star Engine page lists "Dynamic weather" under 4.8 tech, but its citation is the 2023 CitizenCon video and the actual 4.8 patch notes don't support it — treat the wiki row as unreliable.
- No implementation details (resolution, simulation grid, channel formats, runtime vs baked cadence) have been published anywhere. "Physical simulation" is a marketing-level claim; the demo footage is consistent with anything from a coarse advected coverage field to authored sequences.

## Finding 3 — The shipped volumetric cloud system (2020–2023, from CIG monthly reports — hard engineering statements)

Chronology assembled from Star Citizen Monthly Reports (Frankfurt engine/R&D team, all primary):

- **Aug 2020**: volumetric clouds R&D begins; code restructured to share between planet-atmosphere and cloud shaders; cloud raymarch results injected into the **unified atmosphere raymarcher** (clouds and atmosphere are one integrated raymarch pipeline, like Stellar Nomad's goal-state).
- **Nov/Dec 2020**: initial draft of planetary-scale volumetric cloud rendering; volumetric cloud shadows; work on **localized cloud shaping**.
- **Jan 2021**: cloud shadow maps switched to an efficient storage scheme; shadow-map generation supports **incremental updates to amortize per-frame cost** (same amortization idea as Guerrilla's light grid and the repo's light volume).
- **Feb 2021**: cloud *shaping* completed and handed to artists for prototyping; believable **multi-scatter approximation** added; cloud shading includes atmosphere-based ambient light (ground-reflected light deferred).
- **Mar 2021**: **tint support "for coloring clouds for exotic planets"**; multi-scatter now accounts for atmosphere optical depth along shadow rays; shadow refinement; raymarch optimizations; empty-space skipping begun.
- **Apr 2021**: THE authoring statement — artists can specify a **custom set of volume textures per planet** to shape clouds, "as not every planet has clouds resembling those on Earth". SDF generation for skipping optimized/refined.
- **May 2021**: **density queries** (gameplay/VFX can ask "is there cloud here?") and **scattering queries** so transparent/forward-shaded objects integrate with clouds.
- **Jun 2021**: clouds render in secondary views (runtime cubemaps/RTT); **narrow-band SDF computed over cloud coverage** with signed-format mips for empty-space skipping; user-facing quality options added.
- **Jul 2021** (3.14 ships): clouds rendered into runtime cubemaps for ambient lighting; density queries return tint too; cloud shaping tuned for "local vs global read", specifically to fight the **tiled look of cloud detail when viewed from orbit**; the whole reprojection/upsample filter chain revisited so lower-resolution raymarching can always be enabled (full-res marching described as "very taxing").
- **Aug/Sep/Oct 2021**: reprojection + filtering + denoised cloud depth + upsampling of inscatter/transmittance to full res; clouds cast shadows **onto the entire scene** "as the team start to use them on planets as well" (rollout beyond the gas giant); artist-controllable local cloud-shadow stepping; a quick **rain-cloud rendering prototype**; cloud shadows integrated into the atmosphere → **light shafts**; a prototype of improved multi-scatter for clouds+atmosphere; indirect-lighting LUT generation driven by presence of clouds.
- **Nov 2021–2022**: R&D on motion-vector-free reprojection (failed, kept old scheme); noise reduction on raw raymarch results; Gen12/Vulkan ports ("spherical cloud … shadow rendering" — the cloud layer is treated spherically); ground-albedo fade for objects in high orbit.
- **May/Oct 2023** (pre-CitizenCon 2953): temporal render mode improvements; volumetric shadows from clouds into the atmosphere with lazy updates when camera/sun static; low-sun shadow-map space compression for near-camera detail.
- **CitizenCon 2953 (Oct 2023, StarEngine demo)**: truly-3D volumetric cloud light shafts (explicitly contrasted with post-effect god rays); terrain-following ground fog receiving cloud+terrain volumetric shadows; improved shape-noise blending and **vertical variation**; improved short- and long-distance read with less visible tiling. Work credited to Carsten Wenzel (R&D).
- What is **absent** from every report: any 2D coverage/type/weather map, any weather-driven placement, any cloud-genus system. Authoring vocabulary is exclusively volume textures, shaping parameters, tint, painted/authored cloudscapes ("Hurston cloudscaping" appears as an art task in Inside Star Citizen, Mar 2022).

## Finding 4 — Crusader: how the gas giant's clouds are authored/rendered

- Crusader shipped in Alpha 3.14 (Aug 2021) as the debut of "Cloud Tech"; RSI's own 3.14 page: initial implementation of proprietary volumetric cloud tech, to be expanded to other planets. The roadmap deliverable described it as a bespoke system to render **multi-layered volumetric clouds of varying scale within planetary atmospheres**, built for the gas giant.
- Same tech everywhere: the planetary volumetric clouds **grew out of the earlier "stellar gas cloud" (Lagrange-point nebula) tech** (ISC, Jun 2021), and per the wiki the same technology used on Stanton's planets implemented Crusader. One system, per-planet content — matching the plan's per-planet-config seam.
- Authoring: per-planet custom **volume textures** + tint (Apr/Mar 2021 reports). Art direction for Crusader deliberately wanted clouds "less earth-like, a bit more alien" (ISC dev interview). No simulation; static authored layout ("completely static" per CitizenCon 2954). No public band-table / zonal-jet structure — Crusader is an *aesthetic* gas giant, not a fluid-dynamics one, and is Earth-sized (in-game radius ≈ 7,450 km; a dev Spectrum answer gives 14,900 km diameter + 1,200 km atmosphere; the in-game survey lists 1 atm at 8 km altitude, QT arrival at 90 km — numbers are inconsistent across sources but all far smaller/shallower than a real gas giant).
- Habitable band: breathable, ~16–24 °C at the Orison altitude; players fly through cloud layers around the floating city; below the deck the descent fades into haze/fog (no traversable "core"); community videos of "reaching the core" show fog-wall behavior rather than modeled decks.
- Known artifacts at ship: high-contrast and distance artifacts acknowledged on ISC; post-3.14 work planned on SDF occlusion to reduce artifacting.

## Finding 5 — Orbit-view → in-atmosphere cloud LOD/transition

- CIG does **not** use a separate far-field cloud representation: the same volumetric clouds are raymarched from orbit down to the surface, with (a) temporal reprojection + lower-resolution raymarching + upsampling of inscatter/transmittance, (b) quality tiers, (c) shaping tuned so detail doesn't read as tiled **from orbit** (Jul 2021, explicitly), and (d) less-visible tiling / better long-distance read reiterated at CitizenCon 2953. Zero pop-in claims for planets are central CIG marketing.
- Supporting pieces: cloud rendering into runtime cubemaps (ambient light), spherical cloud shadow rendering, ground-albedo fade tuned for objects in high orbit, atmosphere-integrated fog under the cloud deck.
- Interesting parallel to R8 (LOD coherence): at CitizenCon 2955 CIG said the **orbit appearance of planets is now generated from the same data/textures as the ground** (previously orbital and ground coloring used different data) — for terrain, not clouds, but it is CIG converging on exactly the "one source of truth for far and near field" principle in the plan.

## Finding 6 — Procedural weather-map generation / "ecosystem simulation" angle

- Genesis' generation philosophy is rule-based emergence over stacked data, with **offline simulation** used where physical processes matter (erosion sims → localized data maps; water-flow; biome viability/competition). Biomes are *derived labels* from continuous data — the same "store continuous fields, derive appearance" insight as §3.4. The weather/cloud part extends this to runtime: weather emerges from planetary data (oceans/wind/mountains/temperature), rain appears only under physically plausible conditions, and weather is described as evolving across the planet continuously.
- Nothing has been published about **how** weather maps/fields would be generated for a new planet (no circulation model, no humidity-advection description, no channel/texture formats). The CitizenCon 2954 material is outcome-oriented; the plan's ERA5/ISCCP-grounded predictor recipe (EIS, ω, CAPE) is strictly more specific than anything CIG has shown publicly.
- Non-CIG-primary community writeups of the panels (e.g. sandboxer.org's "Brave New Worlds" article) proved to be low-quality/AI-padded (it "announces" decade-old ships) — do not cite them; the panel transcripts are the reliable source.

## RECOMMENDATIONS

- **Update §3.3 in place**: replace the reconstruction with the two-era picture — (a) shipped: static artist-authored raymarched volumetrics, per-planet 3D volume textures + tint, SDF skip, no weather map, no type system; (b) announced Genesis: biome data sets (height/temperature/humidity/geology/soil/erosion) + a runtime physical weather simulation driving cloud formation/dissipation, unshipped as of 4.8. Drop "precipitation, wind" from the input-map list; add geology/soil/nutrients/erosion; keep wind as a weather-*sim* field.
- **Answer open question §6.6 with "no re-run needed"** — this research closes the gap; CIG has published no engineering detail beyond what is captured here, so no further budget should be spent chasing Genesis internals (the only richer future source would be a GDC/SIGGRAPH talk if CIG ever gives one).
- **Keep the §4 design unchanged** — nothing CIG has shown contradicts it. Notably: CIG validates the "same marcher, per-planet content" seam (§4.9), the unified atmosphere+cloud integration, quality-tier low-res marching + reprojection, and orbit-to-surface single-representation with anti-tiling shaping (relevant to the current far-shell work and ISSUE 2: CIG solved the orbit view by *de-tiling the same volumetrics*, not by a separate baked far field — worth remembering as an alternative if shell/volumetric coherence keeps fighting).
- **Cloud types**: CIG offers no prior art — only "stratus and cumulus" emerging from a sim. The plan's ISCCP-continuous-axes + LUT design remains grounded in Nubis/UE5/MSFS (§3.2/§3.5), not Star Citizen; §3.3 should stop implying CIG has a type system.
- **Gas giant**: CIG's Crusader neither validates nor contradicts the band-table generator — they hand-authored an Earth-sized aesthetic gas giant. The plan's physically-derived band-table remains pioneering; Crusader does support the fog-wall-bottom pattern (descent fades to haze, no under-surface) and the "alien palette via tint" idea (their tint channel exists precisely for exotic planets).
- **Two engineering nuggets worth stealing** from CIG's shipped system, independent of §3.3: (1) narrow-band **SDF over the coverage field with signed mips** for empty-space skipping (relevant to marcher perf beyond the current TSL `If` hoisting); (2) **incremental/lazy cloud-shadow-map updates** when sun/camera move little (relevant to cloudLightVolume amortization).
- Source hygiene: cite the monthly reports and panel-transcript quotes (URLs in findings) rather than wiki/news summaries; the sandboxer.org CitizenCon writeup is AI-padded and should not be used.
